# Day-2 Operations

These pages cover what happens after initial setup — managing the lifecycle of guests, building habits that keep the lab recoverable, and diagnosing problems when they appear.

## In This Section

- [VM Lifecycle](/pages/virtualization/operations/vm-lifecycle) — create, baseline, snapshot, clone, back up, migrate, and retire guests in Proxmox.
- [Best Practices](/pages/virtualization/operations/best-practices) — practical habits for keeping a Proxmox-based lab stable, understandable, and easy to recover.
- [Troubleshooting and Performance](/pages/virtualization/operations/troubleshooting) — what to check when VMs or containers are slow, fail to boot, lose network access, or behave strangely under load.
- [Vagrant](/pages/virtualization/operations/vagrant) — repeatable, ephemeral VMs from a single file: the `vagrant up` and `vagrant destroy` workflow for development and experimentation.

## How To Use It

Read VM Lifecycle first if you are setting up new guests. Use Best Practices as a checklist once the lab is running. Return to Troubleshooting whenever something breaks.
